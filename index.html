Business Structure — What’s At Risk vs What’s Protected

Most people start a business without understanding what is actually at risk. The way your business is structured determines your level of exposure, protection, and long-term opportunity.

Sole Proprietor — No Structure

As a sole proprietor, you and your business are legally the same.

Using Your SSN

Your business operates under your personal Social Security Number (SSN). There is no separation between you and your business activity.

  • Your personal bank account can be affected
  • Your car can be affected
  • Your home may be at risk depending on the situation
  • Your income is directly tied to business activity
  • Business debt can follow you personally
  • Business debts and obligations may need to be handled through legal processes and can affect your estate

There is no legal separation between you and your business.

LLC — Limited Liability Company

An LLC creates separation between you and your business—but only if it is operated correctly.

Obtain Your EIN

An EIN means Employer Identification Number. It is issued by the IRS. Go to IRS.gov and apply for an EIN directly through the official IRS website.

  • Replaces your SSN for business use
  • Required to open a business bank account
  • Used for business credit
  • Keeps your personal identity separate
  • Helps your business operate as its own entity

What Can Still Go Wrong

  • Mixing personal and business money
  • Personally guaranteeing loans
  • Poor recordkeeping
  • Not operating like a real business

If handled incorrectly, the protection can break down.

Trust + Holding Structure

This structure separates ownership, control, and operations into layers so everything is not tied directly to you.

How It Works

  • Operating LLC: runs the business, handles customers, sales, and daily activity
  • Holding Company: owns the operating LLC and separates ownership from operations
  • Trust: holds ownership of the holding company
  • Final structure: Trust → Holding Company → Operating Business

Simple Way To Think About It

  • Operating LLC = does the work
  • Holding Company = owns the work
  • Trust = owns the ownership

Generational Wealth Protection

The trust layer helps organize ownership so the business is not only built for today, but positioned for long-term control, succession planning, and generational wealth protection.

  • Keeps ownership structured across generations
  • Supports long-term asset organization
  • Creates a system for succession planning
  • Separates ownership from daily operations

This structure does not eliminate risk or taxes automatically, but it creates a stronger framework for managing both.

Business Expenses & Tax Positioning

When your business is structured and operated correctly, certain expenses used for business purposes may be treated as business expenses.

  • Business-use portion of a vehicle, truck, or trailer
  • Equipment and tools
  • Office-related expenses
  • Marketing and branding
  • Website and digital tools

Proper structure allows you to track and manage expenses which may reduce taxable business income.

Expenses must be ordinary, necessary, and used for business purposes. Personal expenses do not automatically qualify.

Business Credibility & Funding Profile (D-U-N-S / Business Credit)

Dun & Bradstreet (D-U-N-S Number) is what makes your business visible to lenders, vendors, and funding systems — without it, your business is not fully recognized.

Most new business owners stop at getting a license or LLC — but that alone does not make your business visible to lenders, vendors, or funding sources.

To move beyond basic setup, your business needs to be recognized in the commercial system used by banks, suppliers, and credit issuers.

  • Establish your business identity in commercial databases
  • Build a profile used by vendors and lenders
  • Position your business for funding opportunities
  • Separate your personal credit from business activity

This is where most businesses fall short — not because they can’t do it, but because no one shows them how.
