Business Structure — What’s At Risk vs What’s Protected

Most people start a business without understanding what is actually at risk. The way your business is structured determines your level of exposure, protection, and long-term opportunity.

Sole Proprietor — No Structure

As a sole proprietor, you and your business are legally the same.

Using Your SSN

In this setup, your business operates under your personal SSN, which means your personal identity is directly tied to your business activity.

  • Your personal bank account can be affected
  • Your car can be affected
  • Your home can be at risk depending on the situation
  • Your personal income can be impacted
  • Business debts and obligations may need to be handled through legal processes and can affect your estate

LLC — Limited Liability Company

An LLC creates a legal separation between you and your business, but only if you operate it correctly.

Obtain Your EIN

An EIN means Employer Identification Number. It is issued by the IRS. Go to IRS.gov and apply for an EIN directly through the official IRS website.

  • Replaces your SSN for business use
  • Helps open a business bank account
  • Helps apply for business credit
  • Keeps your SSN private
  • Helps your business operate as its own entity

If you mix personal and business money, personally guarantee loans, fail to keep records, or do not operate as a real business, the separation can be weakened.

Trust + Holding Structure

A structured setup separates ownership, control, and operations into different layers so everything is not tied directly to you.

How It Works

  • Operating LLC: runs the business, handles customers, sales, and daily activity
  • Holding Company: owns the operating LLC and separates ownership from operations
  • Trust: holds ownership of the holding company
  • Final structure: Trust → Holding Company → Operating Business

Generational Wealth Protection

The trust layer helps organize ownership so the business is not only built for today, but positioned for long-term control, succession planning, and generational wealth protection.

  • Helps keep ownership organized beyond one person
  • Creates a clearer structure for family succession planning
  • Supports long-term asset organization and control
  • Helps separate daily business activity from ownership planning
  • Supports generational wealth planning and long-term asset organization

This does not automatically eliminate taxes, debt, or risk. A trust must be set up, maintained, and reviewed properly.

Business Expenses & Tax Positioning

When your business is structured and operated correctly, certain expenses used for business purposes may be treated as business expenses. Examples may include the business-use portion of a vehicle, truck, trailer, office expenses, equipment, marketing, branding, website tools, and digital tools.

Expenses must be ordinary, necessary, and used for business purposes. Personal expenses do not automatically count. The goal is not to spend money just to spend—it is to track, separate, and manage expenses correctly.

The level of structure you choose determines how exposed or protected your business and personal life may be.
